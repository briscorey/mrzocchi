  - topic: Ratio & Proportion
    terms:
    - term: Ratio
      emoji: ⚖️
      zh: 比
      definition: A comparison of two quantities using a colon. e.g. 3:5 means '3 to 5'.
    - term: Simplify
      emoji: ✂️
      zh: 化简
      definition: Divide both parts of a ratio by their HCF. e.g. 12:8 = 3:2.
    - term: Equivalent Ratio
      emoji: 🟰
      zh: 等值比
      definition: Ratios with the same value. 2:3 = 4:6 = 6:9 (multiply both parts by the same number).
    - term: Proportion
      emoji: 📊
      zh: 比例
      definition: 'A statement that two ratios are equal. Also: a part expressed as a fraction of the whole.'
    - term: Direct Proportion
      emoji: 📈
      zh: 正比例
      definition: As one quantity increases, the other increases at the same rate. y = kx.
    - term: Constant of Proportionality
      emoji: 🔑
      zh: 比例常数
      definition: The fixed multiplier k in y = kx. Found by dividing y by x.
    - term: Unitary Method
      emoji: 1️⃣
      zh: 归一法
      definition: Find the value of ONE unit first, then multiply. e.g. 5 costs $15 → 1 costs $3 → 8 costs $24.
    - term: Scale
      emoji: 📏
      zh: 比例尺
      definition: The ratio between a drawing/model and real life. e.g. 1:50000 means 1 cm = 500 m.
    - term: Scale Factor
      emoji: 🔍
      zh: 比例因子
      definition: The multiplier used to enlarge or reduce. Scale factor 3 means every length × 3.
    - term: Rate
      emoji: ⏱️
      zh: 速率
      definition: A ratio comparing two quantities with different units. e.g. 60 km/h, $5/kg.
    - term: Best Buy
      emoji: 🛒
      zh: 最优购买
      definition: Finding which option gives the most for the least money by comparing unit prices.

  quiz:
  - q: Simplify the ratio 20:12.
    a: 5:3 — divide both by HCF (4).
  - q: Share £45 in the ratio 2:7.
    a: Total parts = 9. One part = £5. So £10 and £35.
  - q: 5 books cost $30. How much do 8 books cost?
    a: $48 — unit cost = $6, then 8 × $6 = $48.
  - q: y is directly proportional to x. When x=4, y=20. Find k.
    a: k = y/x = 20/4 = 5. So y = 5x.
  - q: Using y = 5x, find y when x = 9.
    a: y = 5 × 9 = 45.
  - q: A map scale is 1:25000. 6 cm on the map = ? metres in reality.
    a: 6 × 25000 = 150,000 cm = 1,500 m = 1.5 km.
  - q: Convert the ratio 7:3 to the form 1:n.
    a: 'Divide both by 7: 1 : 3/7 = 1 : 0.43 (2 d.p.).'
  - q: Increase 60 by 20%.
    a: 20% of 60 = 12. So 60 + 12 = 72.
  - q: Decrease 150 by 30%.
    a: 30% of 150 = 45. So 150 − 45 = 105.
  - q: A car uses 5L per 80km. How much fuel for 200km?
    a: 200 ÷ 80 = 2.5. So 5 × 2.5 = 12.5 litres.
  - q: 'Common mistake: ''Ratio 4:5 means 4/5 of the total.'' Explain the error.'
    a: 4:5 has 9 total parts. The first share is 4/9, NOT 4/5. The denominator is the total parts, not the second number.
  - q: Speed = distance ÷ time. A train covers 420km in 3.5 hours. Find the speed.
    a: 420 ÷ 3.5 = 120 km/h.
  - q: Explain the unitary method with an example.
    a: Find the cost of 1 item first. e.g. 6 pens = $18 → 1 pen = $3 → 10 pens = $30.
  - q: Why must you multiply BOTH parts of a ratio by the same number to keep it equivalent?
    a: Multiplying both parts preserves the relationship. Changing only one side makes it a different comparison.

      emoji: ➗
      zh: 指数除法法则

      emoji: 0️⃣
      zh: 零指数

  quiz:
  - q: 'Calculate: (−8) × (−4)'
    a: 32 — negative × negative = positive.
  - q: 'Calculate: (−15) ÷ 3'
    a: −5 — negative ÷ positive = negative.
  - q: What is 2⁵?
    a: 32 — that's 2×2×2×2×2.
  - q: What is √196?
    a: 14 — because 14 × 14 = 196.
  - q: 'Simplify: 3⁴ × 3²'
    a: '3⁶ = 729 — add indices: 4+2=6.'
  - q: 'Simplify: 7⁸ ÷ 7⁵'
    a: '7³ = 343 — subtract indices: 8−5=3.'
  - q: What is 10⁰?
    a: 1 — any non-zero number to the power 0 equals 1.
  - q: Express 84 as a product of prime factors.
    a: 84 = 2² × 3 × 7.
  - q: Find the HCF of 36 and 48.
    a: 12 — 36=2²×3², 48=2⁴×3. HCF=2²×3=12 (lowest shared powers).
  - q: Find the LCM of 36 and 48.
    a: 144 — LCM=2⁴×3²=144 (highest powers).
  - q: Write 0.00045 in standard form.
    a: 4.5 × 10⁻⁴ — move decimal 4 places right.
  - q: Write 6.2 × 10⁵ as an ordinary number.
    a: 620,000 — move decimal 5 places right.
  - q: 'Common mistake: ''2³ × 2⁴ = 2¹².'' Explain the error.'
    a: You ADD indices when multiplying same base, not multiply them. 2³ × 2⁴ = 2⁷ = 128.
  - q: 'Common mistake: ''√16 + √9 = √25 = 5.'' Why is this wrong?'
    a: You can't add under separate roots. √16 + √9 = 4 + 3 = 7 (not √25 = 5).
  - q: Why is standard form useful?
    a: It makes very large/small numbers readable and comparable. e.g. 0.000000003 m = 3 × 10⁻⁹ m is much clearer.
