quiz:
  - q: "What is a force?"
    a: "A push or pull that can change an object's speed, direction, or shape. Measured in Newtons (N)."
  - q: "Name TWO contact forces and TWO non-contact forces."
    a: "Contact: friction, air resistance, tension. Non-contact: gravity, magnetism."
  - q: "What is the difference between mass and weight?"
    a: "Mass = amount of matter (kg), doesn't change. Weight = force of gravity (N), changes with location. W = m × g."
  - q: "What happens when forces on an object are balanced?"
    a: "The object stays still OR continues moving at constant speed. No change in motion."
  - q: "What is the resultant force if 80 N acts right and 50 N acts left?"
    a: "80 - 50 = 30 N to the right (unbalanced → object accelerates right)."
  - q: "State Newton's First Law."
    a: "An object stays still or moves at constant speed unless an unbalanced force acts on it."
  - q: "State Newton's Second Law and the formula."
    a: "Force = mass × acceleration (F = ma). More force → more acceleration. More mass → less acceleration."
  - q: "State Newton's Third Law."
    a: "Every action has an equal and opposite reaction. Forces act on DIFFERENT objects."
  - q: "What is friction?"
    a: "A force that opposes motion between two surfaces. Converts kinetic energy to heat."
  - q: "What is terminal velocity?"
    a: "When air resistance equals weight. Forces are balanced → object falls at constant speed."
  - q: "Why does opening a parachute slow a skydiver down?"
    a: "It increases surface area → more air resistance → new terminal velocity is much slower."
  - q: "Calculate: F = 12 kg × 3 m/s²"
    a: "F = 36 N"
  - q: "A 60 kg person stands on Earth (g = 10 N/kg). What is their weight?"
    a: "W = 60 × 10 = 600 N"
