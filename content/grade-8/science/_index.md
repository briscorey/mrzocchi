quiz:
  - q: "What is an ecosystem?"
    a: "A community of living organisms interacting with each other AND their non-living environment."
  - q: "Name THREE biotic and THREE abiotic factors."
    a: "Biotic: plants, animals, bacteria. Abiotic: water, sunlight, temperature."
  - q: "What is the difference between a habitat and a niche?"
    a: "Habitat = where it lives. Niche = its role in the ecosystem (what it eats, how it behaves)."
  - q: "What is the difference between a food chain and a food web?"
    a: "Food chain = single pathway. Food web = all interconnected food chains. Webs are more realistic."
  - q: "Which way do arrows point in a food chain?"
    a: "From prey to predator — in the direction of ENERGY FLOW."
  - q: "What is the 10% rule?"
    a: "Only ~10% of energy passes from one trophic level to the next. 90% is lost as heat through respiration."
  - q: "Why do food chains rarely have more than 4-5 levels?"
    a: "Not enough energy left after 4-5 transfers — each level only gets 10% of the previous."
  - q: "What is mutualism? Give an example."
    a: "Both organisms benefit. Example: bees get nectar, flowers get pollinated."
  - q: "What is a trophic cascade?"
    a: "A chain reaction through a food web when a species is added or removed."
  - q: "What happened when wolves were removed from Yellowstone?"
    a: "Elk overgrazed → vegetation declined → erosion increased → rivers changed course → habitat lost."
  - q: "Name THREE human threats to ecosystems."
    a: "Deforestation, pollution, overfishing, invasive species, climate change."
  - q: "What is biodiversity and why does it matter?"
    a: "Variety of species in an ecosystem. Higher biodiversity = more stable, healthier ecosystem."
  - q: "What is the role of decomposers?"
    a: "Break down dead organisms and return nutrients to the soil. Essential for nutrient cycling."
  - q: "Producers capture 10,000 kJ. How much reaches the tertiary consumer?"
    a: "10,000 → 1,000 → 100 → 10 kJ (applying 10% rule three times)."
