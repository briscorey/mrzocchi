  - topic: Place Value & Operations
    terms:
    - term: Place Value
      zh: 位值
      emoji: 🔢
      definition: The value of a digit based on its position in a number. e.g. the 5 in 3,500 represents 500.
    - term: Integer
      zh: 整数
      emoji: 🔵
      definition: Any whole number — positive, negative, or zero. e.g. −3, 0, 7.
    - term: BIDMAS
      zh: 运算顺序
      emoji: 📋
      definition: Brackets, Indices, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction — the order of operations.
    - term: Index / Power
      zh: 指数/幂
      emoji: 📈
      definition: A small number written above and to the right showing how many times to multiply. e.g. 2³ = 2×2×2 = 8.
    - term: Estimate
      zh: 估算
      emoji: 🎯
      definition: An approximate answer found by rounding numbers before calculating.
    - term: Factor
      zh: 因数
      emoji: 🔗
      definition: A number that divides exactly into another number. e.g. factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.
    - term: Multiple
      zh: 倍数
      emoji: ✖️
      definition: The result of multiplying a number by an integer. e.g. multiples of 3 are 3, 6, 9, 12...
    - term: Prime Number
      zh: 质数
      emoji: ⭐
      definition: 'A number with exactly 2 factors: 1 and itself. e.g. 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13.'
    - term: HCF
      zh: 最大公因数
      emoji: 🔝
      definition: Highest Common Factor — the largest number that divides exactly into two or more numbers.
    - term: LCM
      zh: 最小公倍数
      emoji: 🔄
      definition: Lowest Common Multiple — the smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.
    - term: Negative Number
      zh: 负数
      emoji: ➖
      definition: A number less than zero, written with a minus sign. e.g. −5, −12.
    - term: Absolute Value
      zh: 绝对值
      emoji: 📏
      definition: The distance a number is from zero on the number line, ignoring the sign. |−7| = 7.

  quiz:
  - q: What is the value of the 4 in 45,209?
    a: 40,000 — the 4 is in the ten-thousands place.
  - q: Round 3,847 to the nearest hundred.
    a: 3,800 — the tens digit is 4 (less than 5), so round down.
  - q: 'Calculate: 6 + 2 × 5'
    a: '16 — multiplication before addition: 2 × 5 = 10, then 6 + 10 = 16.'
  - q: 'Calculate: (8 − 3)² + 1'
    a: '26 — brackets first: 5, then index: 25, then add 1 = 26.'
  - q: What is −4 + 9?
    a: 5 — start at −4, move 9 to the right = 5.
  - q: What is −6 − (−2)?
    a: '−4 — subtracting a negative is adding: −6 + 2 = −4.'
  - q: List all factors of 24.
    a: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24.
  - q: Is 51 prime? Explain.
    a: No — 51 = 3 × 17, so it has more than 2 factors.
  - q: Find the HCF of 18 and 24.
    a: '6 — factors of 18: 1,2,3,6,9,18. Factors of 24: 1,2,3,4,6,8,12,24. Largest common: 6.'
  - q: Find the LCM of 6 and 8.
    a: '24 — multiples of 6: 6,12,18,24. Multiples of 8: 8,16,24. Smallest common: 24.'
  - q: What is 5³?
    a: 125 — that's 5 × 5 × 5.
  - q: Estimate 48 × 21 by rounding both numbers.
    a: ≈ 50 × 20 = 1,000.
  - q: 'Order from smallest: 3, −1, 0, −5, 2'
    a: −5, −1, 0, 2, 3.
  - q: A lift starts at floor 3, goes down 7 floors. What floor?
    a: Floor −4 (3 − 7 = −4, four floors below ground).
  - q: 'Common mistake: ''6 + 2 × 3 = 24.'' Explain the error.'
    a: 'The student added first (6+2=8) then multiplied (8×3=24). BIDMAS says multiply first: 2×3=6, then 6+6=12.'
  - q: Why is zero neither positive nor negative?
    a: Zero is the boundary between positive and negative numbers. It has no direction — it's not above or below zero, it IS zero.
  - q: Give a real-life example where negative numbers are useful.
    a: Temperature (−5°C), bank overdraft (−$50), below sea level (−200m), lifts going underground (floor −2).

  glossary:
  - topic: Fractions
    terms:
    - term: Fraction
      zh: 分数
      emoji: 🍕
      definition: A number representing part of a whole, written as numerator/denominator. e.g. 3/4.
    - term: Numerator
      zh: 分子
      emoji: ⬆️
      definition: The top number in a fraction — how many parts you have.
    - term: Denominator
      zh: 分母
      emoji: ⬇️
      definition: The bottom number in a fraction — how many equal parts the whole is divided into.
    - term: Equivalent Fractions
      zh: 等值分数
      emoji: 🟰
      definition: Fractions that look different but have the same value. e.g. 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6.
    - term: Simplify
      zh: 化简
      emoji: ✂️
      definition: Divide numerator and denominator by their HCF to make the fraction as simple as possible.
    - term: Improper Fraction
      zh: 假分数
      emoji: 📈
      definition: A fraction where the numerator is larger than the denominator. e.g. 7/4.
    - term: Mixed Number
      zh: 带分数
      emoji: 🔀
      definition: A whole number and a fraction combined. e.g. 1¾.
    - term: LCD
      zh: 最小公分母
      emoji: 🔗
      definition: Lowest Common Denominator — the smallest denominator shared by two fractions. Needed for adding/subtracting.
    - term: Reciprocal
      zh: 倒数
      emoji: 🔄
      definition: A fraction flipped upside down. The reciprocal of 3/4 is 4/3. Used in division (Keep-Change-Flip).
  - topic: Decimals & Percentages
    terms:
    - term: Decimal
      zh: 小数
      emoji: 🔹
      definition: A number using a decimal point to show values less than one. e.g. 0.75 = three quarters.
    - term: Percentage
      zh: 百分比
      emoji: 💯
      definition: A number out of 100. 'Per cent' means 'out of 100'. e.g. 75% = 75/100 = 0.75.
    - term: Convert
      zh: 转换
      emoji: 🔄
      definition: 'Change from one form to another: fraction ↔ decimal ↔ percentage.'

    a: 6/20 = 3/10 — multiply across then simplify.
  - q: 'Calculate: 2/3 ÷ 4/5'
    a: '10/12 = 5/6 — Keep-Change-Flip: 2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6.'
  - q: What is 1/3 of 45?
    a: '15 — ''of'' means multiply: 45 ÷ 3 = 15.'
  - q: Convert 2¾ to an improper fraction.
    a: 11/4 — 2 × 4 = 8, then 8 + 3 = 11. So 11/4.
  - q: 'Which is larger: 3/7 or 5/12?'
    a: '3/7 — convert to same denominator (84): 36/84 vs 35/84.'
  - q: What is 15% of 80?
    a: 12 — 10% is 8, 5% is 4, so 15% = 8 + 4 = 12.
  - q: A shirt costs $40. It's 25% off. What is the sale price?
    a: $30 — discount = 25% of 40 = $10. Sale price = 40 − 10 = $30.
  - q: Explain the 'Keep-Change-Flip' rule.
    a: 'To divide fractions: Keep the first fraction, Change ÷ to ×, Flip the second fraction. Then multiply across.'
  - q: 'Common mistake: ''1/3 + 1/4 = 2/7.'' Explain the error.'
    a: 'You CANNOT add numerators and denominators separately. You need a common denominator first: 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12.'
  - q: Explain why dividing by a fraction makes a number bigger.
    a: '''How many halves fit in 3?'' → 6. Dividing by 1/2 is the same as multiplying by 2. The smaller the fraction you divide by, the bigger the answer.'
  - q: A pizza has 8 slices. You eat 3/8. Your friend eats 25% of the pizza. Who ate more?
    a: 'You: 3/8 = 0.375 = 37.5%. Friend: 25%. You ate more.'

    a: The median isn't affected by outliers. A very high or low value pulls the mean but not the median.

    a: 1/6 — one favourable outcome out of 6 equally likely outcomes.
  - q: A spinner has 3 red, 2 blue, 5 green sections. What is P(blue)?
    a: 2/10 = 1/5 — there are 2 blue out of 10 total sections.
  - q: What does P = 1 mean?
    a: The event is certain to happen. Probability ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).
  - q: You flip a coin 100 times and get 47 tails. What is the experimental P(tails)?
    a: 47/100 = 0.47 — experimental probability = successes ÷ total trials.
  - q: Is rolling a 7 on a standard die possible?
    a: No — a standard die has faces 1–6. P(7) = 0 (impossible).
